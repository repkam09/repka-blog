## Bubbles

Yesterday I discovered [bubbles.town](https://bubbles.town), a website that aims to help the discoverability of small independent personal blogs.

From their `/about` page:

> *Somewhere out there, someone wrote a really good blog post today. You'll probably never find it. Google won't show it to you. Social media buried it under engagement bait. Bubbles tries to surface it. Community voting applied to thousands of personal, independent blogs, with identity and discussion routed through the Fediverse.*

I read through many of the blog posts and found a lot of interesting content that I would not have discovered otherwise.

This led me down a bit of a rabbit hole exploring related indie and small web projects. It is very refreshing to see that this sort of movement exists, I'll have to spend some more time here and get my own website and blogs up and running!

- [IndieWeb](https://indieweb.org/): We are a community of independent and personal websites based on the principles of: owning your domain and using it as your primary online identity, publishing on your own site first (optionally elsewhere), and owning your content.

- [Kagi Small Web](https://blog.kagi.com/small-web): Kagi Small Web offers a fresh approach by promoting recently published content from the "small web." they gather new content, published within the last week, from a handpicked list of blogs and surface it via their search engine and APIs.
